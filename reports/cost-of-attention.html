Cost of Attention

Executive Summary

This article explores how human attention has become a quantifiable, tradable asset and the foundation of the attention economy. Mobile devices and platforms compete to harvest this finite resource, with Apple, Meta (Instagram), and TikTok at the center of the value chain. In 2025, every minute of user focus is worth 1.24¢ (Instagram) or 0.43¢ (TikTok). Meta is nearly 3x more efficient at monetizing attention compared to TikTok. This difference is the Monetization Gap, a central dynamic in the market.

Illustration: The Attention Value Chain

Attention flows from the user's mind through hardware Gatekeepers (Apple) to software Harvesters (Meta, TikTok), turning cognitive time into dollars via ad technology and user engagement. Apple collects a 'tax' via its App Store, while Meta and TikTok fight for time and monetization yield.

  • Apple: Hardware access, regulator, and tax collector
  • MetaInstagram: Monetization machine, highest revenue/minute
  • TikTok: User engagement leader, rapid attention capture

Risk & Strategic Implications

  • Regulatory risk: Privacy laws (e.g. GDPR, Apple's ATT) can cap the inventory of attention platforms may harvest
  • Mental health backlash: User fatigue and regulation may reduce total available attention inventory
  • Macro risk: Ad spend is elastic, directly tied to broader economic cycles
  • Platform risk: Paradigm shifts (e.g. AI companions, AR/VR) could redefine attention capture

Conclusion & Forward Thesis

The future of the attention economy lies in platforms' ability to not only capture time, but also emotional state and focus. Meta monetizes best, TikTok engages best, Apple profits by regulating the space; the next era will reward platforms that can deepen the capture and monetization of user attention.
